Freshman Support Re-branding

During my work for Freshman Support, I was faced with condensing an extreme amount of information from financial aid resources and making it easier to understand as a student with little financial experience. I also assisted with the UX of the rest of the site, and I designed the accompanying mobile site, taking the webpages of the other designers, and creating a seamless mobile experience.

Sun Galoshes

Sun Galoshes is a weather app that I'm designing and developing. It takes a minimalist approach to the icons, and the information becomes key. This project required a lot of information architecture, and most of the user experience work was focused on how the large amount of information would be shown. Weather information is extremely data and number driven, and I chose to make the key components the temperature and rain chance, with the extra information being available in a pop-up dialog. I'm also looking into the options available for a weather map.

Seventy Days

Scoliosis is the documentation of the healing process of my spine after surgery to correct scoliosis. The project consisted of 70 days of pictures of my back. The white poster displays these pictures with the final x-ray featured on the left, and the other addresses the mental changes that stemmed from such a radical change. The black poster utilizes the ruler and the bolded typographical elements to mimic a straightened spine, with the text overhange recalling the shape of the curvature before surgery.

Mintor

Mintor is an app that helps prospective, current, and former students manage their finances and estimate the information they need for their student loans. I worked on this with a partner, and completed the UX and the visual design. We wanted something that would be easy to navigate, would be useful for multiple stages of the college experience, and that would make the financial information a little less intimidating.

Hello.

Hello is an app that helps people learn basic phrases while traveling. I researched phrases that would be helpful while traveling, while staying with a minimal approach. My chosen words were hello, goodbye, yes, no, and the phrase “how much?” The app is designed for Android. The second version of the app features the word “hello” floating across the screen in different languages. When they bump into each other, they change color, indicating the way cultures can influence each other. This approaches the idea of the first app symbolically, and the user can move the words around and impact the environment themselves. This is one of the apps I programmed, and the code can by found on Github.

Esperu

Esperu is a calendar that teaches the user the auxiliary language Esperanto in the course of the year. The language is fairly modular, so each week is separated into a part of speech, and the accompanying grammar booklet teaches the simple grammar. I expanded upon research from the Hello project on this to develop a weekly system through which to learn a language, which is made possible with the structure of Esperanto. This was also an experiment on how to design a flow and experience in a physical product. This system is one that can be constantly modified, and the project underwent modifications for the Senior Exhibition.
