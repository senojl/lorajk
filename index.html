Freshman Support Re-branding

While working for the start-up, Freshman Support, I designed the financial portion of the site with a team of 5 designers all contributing to the website. As that was going on throughout the summer, I also designed the mobile site taking the visual language of the other designers and converting it to a mobile layout. Some of the other tasks I had was to create icons, develop UI and UX, and contribute variants of the home page. My idea was not chosen for the home page ultimately, and I learned a tremendous amount about working with other designers through this process.

Seventy Days

Scoliosis is the documentation of the healing process of my spine after surgery to correct scoliosis. The project consisted of 70 days of pictures of my back. One poster displays this, and the other addresses the mental changes that stemmed from such a radical change.

Mintor

Mintor is an app that helps prospective, current, and former students manage their finances and estimate the information they need for their student loans. I worked on this with a partner, and completed the UX and the visual design. We wanted something that would be easy to navigate, would be useful for multiple stages of the college experience, and that would make the financial information a little less intimidating.

Exhibition

I was part of the marketing team for the University of Texas 2014 BFA Design show. This involved a small team that decided the look and feel of the show and designed any branding material for the show. I helped develop the pattern that became the brand for the show, and I designed the invitation that went out to local studios and designers. Three of my projects were in the show, Esperu, Seventy Days, and Hello. Last picture is courtesy of the VAC and the website for the show can be found at http://designut.org/seniors2014.

Hello.

Hello is an app that helps people learn basic phrases while traveling. The app is designed for Android. The second version of the app features the word “hello” floating across the screen in different languages. When they bump into each other, they change color, indicating the way cultures can influence each other. This approaches the idea of the first app symbolically, and the user can move the words around and impact the environment themselves. This is one of the apps I programmed, and the code can by found on Github.

Hello.

Esperu is a calendar that teaches the user the auxiliary language Esperanto in the course of the year. The language is fairly modular, so each week is separated into a part of speech, and the accompanying grammar booklet teaches the simple grammar. This system is one that can be constantly modified, and the project underwent modifications for the Senior Exhibition.

Flood Campaign Bus Ad

A bus ad designed for the Turn Around, Don't Drown campaign run by the Watershed Protection Department in the City of Austin. The ad was the first prize for a poster contest for elementary and middle school students, and therefore was the main aspect of the ad. This project incorporated the use of existing graphics, the winning posters, and the website being advertised. I worked with the project manager for the campaign closely to come to this final design. The ad was displayed on the back of CapMetro buses for a month in March of 2014.
