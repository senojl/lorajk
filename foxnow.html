FOX NOW Android TV

Includes UX/UI Design for Android TV

Android TV

After a team had worked on the FOX NOW Android app, I had the chance to work on the Android TV app. I was the sole designer on the app, while still consulting with my coworkers to ensure consistency between the Android mobile app and TV app. The challenge of this project was to follow the standards set by the mobile app team, while also following standards for Android TV. Most of the app was designed following the existing Android TV standards, heavily relying on the navigation patterns found in the side menu, with some deviation for the show detail page.

It was important while designing the app to look at the existing mobile app and I found an opportunity to heavily feature the show art on the detail page, while maintaining a usable TV grid style. This was also inspired by the mobile app that has rows of contents. The show detail uses the hero art to create visual interest for the page and maintain the different show identities for each detail. In the mobile app, this is done with color and imagery, which is difficult to do on TV. In an early visual iteration, we found many of the show colors to be overwhelming when featured on a TV screen, so I designed the entire app in a dark mode color scheme.

Android TV Cards

Another challenge was the metadata that needed to be featured on the cards for each video in app. On many other Android TV apps, the video title and author/show is shown, while the FOX NOW app required several lines of metadata. I worked to maintain a consistent hierarchy for each card, while featuring all of the information on the card. I feel using the various weights in type to be an easy way to differentiate all the information, while still maintaining the TV app guidelines for text weight and size.
